# Evaluation upgrade plan — supplementary experiments & figures

Purpose: bring the STPS evaluation up to recent ATC/OSDI/NSDI conventions. The
current `article.tex` prose has been reshaped (worst-case-forward headline,
"preserved throughput" framing, RQ-findings lead, takeaway captions) using data
already in `experiment.md` / `experiment_regen.md`. This file lists the **new
figures** and **new experiments** to add in a follow-up pass. Nothing here is
required for the paper to compile today; it is the roadmap to make the eval
competitive.

Model precedent: **Shockwave (NSDI '23)** — a scheduler whose headline is a
worst-case metric while the mean is *preserved*, validated by simulation against
a real reference. Our story maps onto it directly.

Conventions we are matching (source: survey of Shockwave, Pollux OSDI'21,
Sarathi-Serve OSDI'24, Splitwise ISCA'24, TetriInfer):
- lead results with a worst-case/tail metric, frame the mean as preserved;
- replace the one dense comparison table with focused figures (normalized bars
  + CDF + sensitivity lines);
- add a quantified *motivation measurement* before the solution;
- report mechanism overhead, robustness to prediction error, multi-seed error
  bars, and a simulator-fidelity/limitations subsection.

Tooling that already exists (reuse, do not rewrite):
- Interpreter: `/root/miniconda3/envs/snn/bin/python` (conda env `snn`).
- Runners: `script/q0_run.py`, `script/q1_run.py`, `script/q2_run.py`,
  `script/q2_bw_dmax_run.py`, `script/q0_stps_long_tail.py`.
- Plotters: `script/plot_cv_cdf.py` (CDF), `script/q0_stps_long_tail.py`
  (survival curves — already produces `picture/stps_long_tail_*.pdf`).
- Table regen: `script/gen_tables.py` → rewrites `experiment_regen.md`.
- Data dirs: `data/q0/`, `data/q1/`, `data/q2/`. Figures land in `picture/`.
- Priority ranking below: **P0** = do first (cheap, high reviewer value),
  **P1** = strengthens the paper, **P2** = nice-to-have.

## Part A — New / replacement figures (from EXISTING data)

These need no new simulation; they re-plot numbers already in
`experiment.md` / `experiment_regen.md`. Goal: retire the dense 6-cell
`tab:q0_main` as the primary artifact and let figures carry the story.

### FIG-1 (P0) — Per-card NoC-pressure CDF  *(the headline figure)*
- **Type:** CDF / survival of per-card served load (or per-card congestion),
  16 cards, one panel per arrival (Poisson, Bursty). STPS vs the 4 baselines.
- **Why:** this is the single figure that sells the worst-case story — the tail
  (right edge) separates STPS from baselines even where the body overlaps. It is
  the Shockwave FTF-distribution / Pollux fairness-CDF analogue.
- **Source:** per-card load arrays from the 16-card runs (`data/q0/scale16_*`).
  `script/plot_cv_cdf.py main16 16` already computes per-scheduler CDFs — extend
  it to plot the max/min or per-card load distribution, not just CV.
- **Caption takeaway:** "STPS's per-card pressure distribution has a far shorter
  right tail: the busiest card is bounded at 13.5x vs baselines' 24x, while the
  median card is unchanged."
- **Replaces:** the max/min column's role as the worst-case evidence.

### FIG-2 (P0) — Normalized congestion bar chart, 6 cells
- **Type:** grouped bar chart, congestion ratio (and cong-wait) normalized to
  the best baseline = 1.0, error bars = 95% CI. x = {4,16} cards × {Poisson,
  Bursty, Mixed}; bars = 5 schedulers.
- **Why:** small deltas (5-7%) read as legible bars once normalized; "STPS bar
  is below 1.0 in all six groups, no baseline is" is visually the consistency
  claim. Replaces the eye-glazing central table.
- **Source:** `tab:q0_main` numbers already in `experiment.md` (has ±CI).
- **Caption takeaway:** "STPS is the only policy under the best-baseline line in
  every cell; baselines trade leadership."
- **Keep** `tab:q0_main` but move it to an appendix or shrink to congestion +
  worst-card + throughput columns only.

### FIG-3 (P1) — Incremental ablation ("+feature") bar chart
- **Type:** incremental bars: baseline -> +Stage A (macro-dispatch) ->
  +Stage B (phase-shift) = full STPS, annotated with marginal congestion
  reduction and throughput cost at each step. One panel per arrival.
- **Why:** ATC ablation convention (Sarathi Table 4 / Shockwave §8.6 as bars).
  Shows the two stages are complementary and that Stage B alone is expensive.
- **Source:** `tab:q2_phase` rows (`stps-spatial`, `stps-spatial+ps`, baselines
  ±ps) already in `experiment_regen.md`.
- **Caption takeaway:** "Neither stage alone reaches the operating point; only
  the pairing relieves congestion at preserved throughput."

### FIG-4 (P2) — Sensitivity line plots (already partly present)
- `fig:q1_util`, `fig:q1_mix`, and the D_max sweep are already line plots; keep
  them but restyle to a common template (markers, shared legend, takeaway
  captions — done). Consider merging the two Stage-A sweeps into a 2-panel
  figure to save space.

## Part B — New experiments (require running the simulator)

Ordered by reviewer expectation. Each item: what to run, how, expected output,
where it plugs into the paper.

### EXP-1 (P0) — Motivation measurement: quantify the congestion problem
- **Goal:** a TetriInfer-style characterization, placed in §2/§3 (Background),
  that proves the pathology *before* the solution: co-locating bursty tenants on
  a capacity-balanced cluster saturates the per-card NoC even at low mean
  utilisation.
- **Run:** uncapped vs capped comparison on a capacity baseline (BestFit/DRF),
  16 cards, bursty. Measure: (a) per-card instantaneous injection vs BW_max over
  time (time-series), (b) fraction of ticks the busiest card exceeds the cap,
  (c) worst-card/mean pressure as a function of mean utilisation.
  `CARDS=16 ARRIVAL_MODE=bursty BW_MAX=9e5 python script/q0_run.py ...`
  plus an uncapped (`BW_MAX=5e6`) reference; reuse `q2_bw_dmax_run.py` cap sweep.
- **Output:** 1 time-series figure + 1 sentence with the headline number
  (e.g. "at 65% mean utilisation the busiest card's NoC is saturated N% of
  ticks"). This is the figure that justifies why the modest averages matter.
- **Plugs into:** new §2 "Motivation" measurement paragraph + figure.

### EXP-2 (P0) — Mechanism overhead
- **Goal:** reviewers will ask "what does the scheduler itself cost?" Report:
  (a) per-admission scheduler decision latency (Stage 1 O(M) + Stage 2
  O(D_max·H)), wall-clock, vs the baselines' O(M); (b) offline fingerprint
  extraction time per model and artifact size (already ~tens of KB — confirm);
  (c) memory footprint of the forecast timelines.
- **Run:** instrument `schedule/stps.py` `select_card_for_task` + phase-shift
  with a timer; run at 4 and 16 cards, 512-3200 tasks; report mean/p99 decision
  latency in microseconds. Fingerprint cost: time `fingerprint.cli` on the 3
  real traces.
- **Output:** small table (scheduler × decision-latency µs, + fingerprint
  extraction s/model). Compare to per-task admission budget.
- **Plugs into:** new eval subsection "Scheduling Overhead" (~0.3 col).

### EXP-3 (P1) — Robustness to fingerprint / forecast error
- **Goal:** STPS relies on offline fingerprints; reviewers will ask what happens
  when the deployment trace drifts from calibration (Shockwave §8.10 injects
  ±p% prediction noise). Show graceful degradation.
- **Run:** perturb each task's traffic timeline E^(t) and beta by ±{10,25,50}%
  (multiplicative noise / temporal jitter) at scheduling time while the true
  simulated traffic stays fixed; measure congestion ratio & worst-card pressure
  vs noise level. Add a `--fingerprint-noise` knob to the engine or wrap the
  fingerprint loader.
- **Output:** line plot, congestion metric vs noise%, showing STPS stays better
  than baselines until noise is large, then converges to baseline (never worse).
- **Plugs into:** eval subsection "Robustness to Calibration Drift". Directly
  answers the RQ3 limitation currently only hand-waved.

### EXP-4 (P1) — Scalability sweep (card count)
- **Goal:** the worst-card result is scale-dependent (4 cards weak, 16 strong);
  show the trend. Shockwave/Pollux both sweep cluster size.
- **Run:** cards ∈ {4, 8, 16, 32, 64}, task count scaled to hold utilisation,
  bursty. Measure worst-card pressure (max/min) and congestion ratio for STPS vs
  best baseline at each size.
- **Output:** line plot, worst-card pressure vs card count — the STPS-vs-P2C gap
  widening with scale is a strong figure.
- **Plugs into:** RQ1 or a new "Scalability" paragraph; replaces the current
  4-vs-16 two-point comparison with a curve.

### EXP-5 (P1) — Simulator fidelity / validation
- **Goal:** simulation-only papers must defend the model (Shockwave reports
  ~5% fidelity deltas vs a real cluster). We have no CIM hardware, so instead:
  (a) validate the NoC/FIFO injection model against published Darwin3 / Loihi
  NoC bandwidth and latency numbers (cite the hardware papers already in
  references.bib: `ma2024darwin3`, `davies2018loihi`); (b) sensitivity of the
  qualitative ordering to the p75 cap choice (already partly in Appendix A —
  cross-reference); (c) explicit "what the model omits" paragraph (routing,
  arbitration, per-core compute timing).
- **Run:** mostly analysis + one cap-choice sensitivity confirming ordering
  holds at p50/p75/p90 (the regime sweep already covers this — repackage).
- **Output:** a "Threats to Validity / Simulator Fidelity" subsection + a small
  table mapping model parameters to hardware-reported values.
- **Plugs into:** end of eval or a dedicated subsection before Conclusion.

### EXP-6 (P2) — Multi-seed error bars everywhere
- **Goal:** every bar/line figure should carry 95% CI (we already run 5 seeds;
  the CIs exist in the summary CSVs but are not all plotted).
- **Run:** none new — thread the ±CI columns from the summary CSVs into every
  figure (`plot_cv_cdf.py` and the new bar plotters).
- **Output:** error bars on FIG-2, FIG-3, EXP-3/4 plots.
